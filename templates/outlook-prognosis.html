Outlook / Prognosis

Many people live long, active lives with heart disease. Your outlook depends on the type of condition, how early it is found and how well it is managed.

What Affects Prognosis?

  • Type and severity of heart disease
  • Age and other medical conditions such as diabetes or kidney disease
  • How early the condition was diagnosed
  • How well risk factors are controlled
  • Adherence to medicines and follow-up appointments
  • Participation in cardiac rehabilitation or lifestyle programs

Your healthcare provider can explain what your diagnosis means for you personally and how your outlook may change over time.

Living Well With Heart Disease

Heart disease is often a long-term condition, but many people continue to work, travel and enjoy their usual activities with proper management.

  • Follow treatment plans and attend follow-up visits.
  • Ask questions until you understand your condition and medicines.
  • Recognize symptoms that should be reported promptly.
  • Seek support from family, friends, counselors or support groups.
  • Set realistic goals and celebrate progress toward better health.
