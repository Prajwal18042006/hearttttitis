What Is Heart Disease?

Heart disease is a broad term for conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. Understanding the basics can help you protect your heart and work with your healthcare team.

Overview

‚ÄúHeart disease‚Äù is commonly used to describe several different problems with the heart and blood vessels. Many of these conditions are related to a process called atherosclerosis, where fatty deposits (plaque) build up inside the arteries.

Over time, this buildup can narrow or block blood flow to the heart muscle and other organs. When the heart does not get enough oxygen-rich blood, it can lead to chest pain, heart attack, heart failure or other serious complications.

  • Heart disease is common but often preventable.
  • It can affect people of all genders and ages.
  • Healthy habits and medical care can lower your risk.

Common Types of Heart Disease

Different types of heart disease affect the heart in different ways. Many people may have more than one type at the same time.

How Heart Disease Develops

Many forms of heart disease are long-term (chronic) conditions that develop slowly. High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity and lack of exercise can damage blood vessels and the heart over time.

This damage can lead to plaque buildup, stiffening of the arteries and changes in how the heart muscle works. Because this process is gradual, you may not notice symptoms at first. Regular checkups are important, especially if you have risk factors.
