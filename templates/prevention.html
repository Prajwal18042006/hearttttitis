Prevention

You cannot control every risk factor, but many everyday choices can reduce your chances of developing heart disease or having complications.

Heart-Healthy Lifestyle

Small, consistent changes add up over time. You do not need to be perfect. Focus on building habits you can maintain for the long term.

Check and Control Risk Factors

Regular health screenings help detect problems early, when they may be easier to treat.

  • Monitor blood pressure and follow treatment if it is high.
  • Check cholesterol and manage it with diet, exercise and medicines if needed.
  • Screen for diabetes and keep blood sugar in target ranges.
  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced habits.
  • Discuss your family history and personal risk with your provider.
