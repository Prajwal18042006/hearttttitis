When to See a Doctor

Heart symptoms should never be ignored. Use this guide to understand when to call emergency services, contact your provider or schedule a routine visit.

Call Emergency Services Immediately

Dial your local emergency number (such as 911) if you or someone near you experiences:

  • Chest pain, pressure or tightness that lasts more than a few minutes
  • Pain that spreads to the arm, back, neck, jaw or stomach
  • Shortness of breath, especially if sudden or severe
  • Sudden weakness, fainting, heavy sweating or nausea
  • Heart palpitations with dizziness or loss of consciousness

Do not drive yourself to the hospital. Emergency responders can begin treatment on the way and are trained to handle sudden complications.

Contact Your Healthcare Provider Soon

Some symptoms are not emergencies but still require a prompt phone call or visit with your provider.

Call Within 24 Hours If You Notice

  • New or worsening swelling in the legs, ankles or abdomen
  • Weight gain of more than 2 pounds (1 kg) in a day or 5 pounds (2 kg) in a week
  • Increasing shortness of breath with normal activities
  • Heart palpitations that are frequent or bothersome
  • Medication side effects such as dizziness, coughing or rash

Schedule a Follow-up When

  • You finish a course of treatment or start a new medicine
  • It has been more than a year since your last heart checkup
  • You have risk factors such as diabetes, high blood pressure or high cholesterol
  • A close relative was recently diagnosed with early heart disease
  • You want help creating a prevention or lifestyle plan

Prepare for Your Visit

Keep a list of your symptoms, when they began and what makes them better or worse. Bring your medication list, recent test results and questions you want answered. Having this information ready helps your care team respond quickly.

Track Measurements

Write down home blood pressure readings, heart rate and weight trends.

Bring Support

Ask a trusted friend or family member to join you for note-taking and encouragement.

Share Goals

Let your provider know what matters most—work, family, energy or sports—so the plan fits your life.
